ジャンル

Find your genre

少年

Shonen

Action and adventure aimed at younger audiences — training arcs, rivals, and friendship as a superpower.

少女

Shojo

Romance and coming-of-age stories with expressive art and big emotional payoffs.

青年

Seinen

Grittier, more mature storytelling — psychological drama, crime, and slow-burn character studies.

異世界

Isekai

Ordinary people transported to other worlds, from epic fantasy to outright comedy.

日常

Everyday moments made luminous — school clubs, small towns, food, and friendship.

歴史

A very short history

1960s — 1970s

The pioneers

Television anime takes shape with early black-and-white series and the first giant-robot shows, establishing the visual language the medium still speaks today.

1980s

The golden boom

Home video and theatrical films push animation quality to new heights. Science fiction dominates, and anime begins finding audiences far beyond Japan.

1990s — 2000s

The global wave

Landmark TV series and studio films reach worldwide audiences. Late-night broadcast slots open the door for more experimental, adult-oriented work.

2010s — Today

The streaming era

Simulcasts make new episodes available worldwide within hours of airing. Anime becomes one of the most-watched categories of television on the planet.

用語集

Glossary for new fans

OP / ED
The opening and ending theme sequences of a series — often small works of art in their own right.
Sakuga 作画
A standout moment of exceptionally fluid or expressive animation, celebrated frame by frame by fans.
Cour
A broadcast block of roughly 12 to 13 episodes. A "two-cour" show runs about 24 to 26 episodes.
OVA
Original video animation — episodes released directly to home video rather than broadcast on TV.
Seiyuu 声優
A voice actor. Top seiyuu have devoted followings and careers that span decades.
Manga 漫画
Japanese comics — the source material for a large share of anime adaptations.
